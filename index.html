Mumbai

Mumbai (formerly called Bombay) is a densely populated city on India’s west coast. A financial center, it's India's largest city. On the Mumbai Harbour waterfront stands the iconic Gateway of India stone arch, built by the British Raj in 1924. Offshore, nearby Elephanta Island holds ancient cave temples dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva. The city's also famous as the heart of the Bollywood film industry

Some of the historic landmarks in Mumbai

Elephanta Caves

Elephanta Caves – Ancient rock-cut temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, famous for their sculptures and recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Gateway of India

Gateway of India – A historic monument built in 1924 and one of Mumbai's most iconic landmarks overlooking the Arabian Sea.

Marine Drive

Marine Drive – A 3.6 km seaside promenade, known as the Queen's Necklace for its beautiful lights at night.

Top three activities to do in Mumbai

Shopping Paradise

Mumbai is a shopper's paradise, offering everything from bustling street markets to modern malls. Visitors can find trendy fashion, unique souvenirs, handicrafts, and delicious local treats at affordable prices.

Parks in Mumbai

Mumbai is home to beautiful parks like Sanjay Gandhi National Park and Hanging Gardens, offering lush greenery, peaceful surroundings, and a perfect escape from the city's busy life.

Famous Food of Mumbai

Mumbai is famous for its delicious street food, including Vada Pav, Pav Bhaji, Bhel Puri, and Misal Pav. These local dishes offer an authentic taste of the city's vibrant food culture.
